A little bit about myself:

I am a developer of websites, VR, and games. I mostly use C# and Unity, and created a selection of educational VR games and experiences as an apprentice at Sophia Technologies Ltd. I also enjoy making games and VR software in my spare time, and I am currently working on bolstering my knowledge of web development.
I am set to begin a Degree Apprenticeship in Digital and Technology Solutions (Software Development) with GSK and the University of Hertfordshire this September, and in the meantime I am working as a Front of House Staff member at The Bulls Head in Turnford.

Why Software Development?

I want to pursue a career in Software Development because I feel that software is a crucial backbone of our lives and our businesses; They embody human thoughts and ideas in a visual and permanent way. Software is one of the most accessible and far-reaching mediums in the world, and every company would benefit from utlising applications in their operations.
I love problem solving through writing code and seeing the effects of my work visually and instantly, so I can continually tweak and improve my work to make it the best possible version it can be.

Why am I pursuing a Degree Apprenticeship?

Studying a Degree Apprenticeship allows me to gain first-hand practical experience as a software developer, working on real-world projects and using cutting-edge technologies as part of a team, while also working towards a degree to underpin the practical skills I develop with a theoretical understanding of the technologies I will be involved in.
Upon completing the course, I will be a software engineer with multiple years of work experience at a large reputable coporation as well as holding a Bachelor's Degree in the field, which will solidify my career as a developer.
